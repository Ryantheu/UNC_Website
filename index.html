Real-time traffic monitoring

It’s like something you’ve seen in a movie. Since it’s implementation in 2015, a large room in Cary’s Town Hall has been dedicated entirely to real-time traffic monitoring. Top of the line cameras are situated at all of Cary’s major intersections, giving the town the opportunity to respond quicker to emergencies than ever before. The system also helps to collect traffic data and ease traffic when it gets too heavy. There is always an employee whose entire job is to keep an eye on the screens. To find more information about this project, search "Advanced Traffic Management System" on the municipal map.

Smart Crosswalks

Flashing crosswalks are a tool that the Town of Chapel Hill uses to improve safety for its pedestrians. The town has installed these crosswalks on busy, multiple lane roads. Instead of a person having a push a button and wait for a response, new optical and sensing technology provided by Hitachi will recognize when a person is about to step into the crosswalk. The lights on the sign will immediately begin to flash. To find more information about this project, search "Smart Crosswalks" on the municipal map.

City Hall On the Go!

Durham’s city hall is located in its bustling downtown, right next to the Tobacco District, the Durham Bulls Stadium and right around the corner from DPAC. In order to service the more remote communities of Durham, the government has initiated “City Hall on the Go!” Actualized in a giant, RV-looking truck, this program provides many of city hall’s services including the opportunity to pay water bills, internet access for employment research/application and providing other Durham departmental information. To find more information about this project, search "City Hall on the Go" on the municipal map.

About Our Project


Our capstone group partnered with RTCC to build this map application as a visual representation of municipal and university cleantech projects in the Research Triangle area. In addition to gathering all the project data, we also identified resources and advantages that the Triangle has that would be beneficial to any incoming Cleantech industries looking to move here. We wrote a paper summarizing these resources, putting them into the context of innovation hub theory. We compared this region's assets and policies to those of Austin, San Diego, and Berlin to further put the Triangle's work in perpective. Finally we used the report to make recommendations on what the Triangle could be doing to better to turn itself into a global leader in Cleantech.
